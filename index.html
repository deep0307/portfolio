Hello there, I'm a graduate student in Computer Science at University of Southern California (USC) with a background in Algorithms & Data Structures, Software Engineering and Machine Learning. I'm excited to work on cutting-edge projects and collaborate with other professionals that share my interests.

I am deeply passionate about developing websites and applications, with a keen interest in both front-end and back-end development. My enthusiasm extends to data analysis where I excel in identifying patterns and extracting meaningful insights from complex datasets. Furthermore, I possess substantial knowledge in database design and development, enabling me to effectively manage and optimize databases.

I am currently employed as a Algorithm Developer at the Autonomous Networks Research Group, where I collaborate with my mentor, Jared Coleman, an Assistant Professor at Loyola Marymount University. I am working on the SAGA Framework, an open-source library developed in Python designed to run, evaluate, and compare task scheduling algorithms. I built various heterogenous task scheduling algorithms and incorporating various constraints on the algorithm to improve load balancing.

Beyond my coding endeavors, I have a passion for outdoor sports, including soccer, table tennis, and cricket. In my leisure time, I also enjoy diving into the world of anime and engaging in video games.

Thank you for taking the time to understand my interests and background. I am truly excited to explore where my passion for software development and data science will guide my future endeavors.
